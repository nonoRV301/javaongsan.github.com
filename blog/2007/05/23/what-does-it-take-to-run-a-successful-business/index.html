What Does It Take to Run a Successful Business?

May 23rd, 2007

I started blogging fulltime blogging only in May. Like any home based
business owner, I too struggle between getting my work done and enjoying
the comfort of my home.

In 2003, I ran a software development business from home for about 6
months, the toughest part was getting out of bed and getting motivated. In
the end, I have trade my freedom for a more discipline corporate life. This
time round, history are not likely to be repeated. I have gone through 3
years of personal development, education myself on time management, goal
setting, sales & marketing, motivation and running unconventional
businesses.

I put together a 5 steps program to help you start a successful homebased
business
1. Dream
The single most important piece that could break or make your business. You
need a good reason for starting your business. It has to be so important
that you willing to risk your life for. List down every single reason and
be specific. A dream might be to spent more time with your kids, cos you
are so busy with work that you hardly see them.
2. The Action Plan
Here we want to convert your dreams to realistic goals. This is your game
plan. Set a dateline for your goal. A example of a goal might be, “I retire
on 27 june 2006 and spent lots of time with my children because I make 5
thousand dollars a month from my business”. Write down on a piece of paper
and struck where you will it everyday, like on your monitor.
3. Daily habits
Your goals needs to be translated to daily activities like a habit. For my
blog, it translates to how many post everyday by when, down to the minute,
reading up on related topics, researching, studying stats, generating a fix
amount of traffic, making a certain amount of sales etc.
4. Track and Measure
Nothing can be improve unless it can tracked. Record your work and results
everyday. I once hear a salesman record what he wore everyday, he will then
stick to those clothes that are bringing him results.
5. Don’t give up
The only reason why people failed In sports, work or businesses is simple
they gave up. If you keeping going, you get it done eventually.
