Google PR Update

Aug 18th, 2007

The Google PR update happen recently. I notice a few of the site I visited drop in their PR. A number of blog have PR reduce to o or nothing. My blog PR went from 3 to 1 to nothing. I believe that the algorithm have been updated, they always do, but then we will not know what was updated. I hope that some day I be able to see the codes behind the search engine.

PageRank Explained (extract from http://www.google.com/technology/)
PageRank relies on the uniquely democratic nature of the web by using its vast link structure as an indicator of an individual page’s value. In essence, Google interprets a link from page A to page B as a vote, by page A, for page B. But, Google looks at considerably more than the sheer volume of votes, or links a page receives; for example, it also analyzes the page that casts the vote. Votes cast by pages that are themselves “important” weigh more heavily and help to make other pages “important.” Using these and other factors, Google provides its views on pages’ relative importance.

Of course, important pages mean nothing to you if they don’t match your query. So, Google combines PageRank with sophisticated text-matching techniques to find pages that are both important and relevant to your search. Google goes far beyond the number of times a term appears on a page and examines dozens of aspects of the page’s content (and the content of the pages linking to it) to determine if it’s a good match for your query.

My link build wasn’t intensive, and using link exchange sites doesn’t help either. To get better Pr you need to be link by sites that have quality content, higher PR than yours and of the same topic. If I am a normal web master I would not be bother by page rank updates, however if you are involved in Paid Post program like Payperpost you need higher PR to get paid better. Most of these program uses Google PR and Alexa ranking as bench marks. Alexa is is measurement of your blog from user who have a Alexa toolbar plug-in on their browser when they surf your sites. The measurement are inaccurate and difficult to improve on, many web site owner do not include Alexa as a gauge of their site traffic. Google PR is more accurate but harder to archive, the only thing that is made know is to improve the content of your site and the backlinks form quality sites.

As my page rank comes down, blogging for services like Payperpost becomes harder. Using ad networks is not a good solution with a site that has only less than 200 page views a day. I would be looking for a better way to make money from my blog.
