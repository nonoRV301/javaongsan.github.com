How Many Blog Can You Write On?

Jan 30th, 2008 | Comments

There are lots of debate on the number of blog one should have. Most successful bloggers are likely to write only one blog and it seems like you only need one blog to be profitable. What happens when your blog did not take off? Maybe there are no readers, not enough traffic, or simply a bad niche? I do believe that if you work hard on one blog alone, you eventually get there, but what about when you have not make it yet. Your blog have too little traffic or readers to sell advertisement and your Adsense earning is only a few cents for entire month.

When I started, I started 5 blogs and only kept 2. Overtime I have started new blogs, keep some going and shutdown some. I was working on a full-time job back then, I still found time to work each blog daily. There are many benefits of starting multiple blogs. You have a sharper learning curve, you save time by experimenting different things on different blogs. After a period of time, you will shutdown the less preforming ones and work on the better performing ones. You choose to let this discarded blog float, where you might find some use for them in the future or use it to link to some of your websites, you might even be able to sell links or advertising spaces on them.

While most of my blog were started with free host, I have found that time and time again that if you host your own blog, you get better traffic fast. I have one blog lunch that will gain organic traffic the next day. Of course there a lot more SEO tactics involved but then again you can’t use them on free hosts like blogger.

I doÃ‚ recommend you start as many blogs as you can, even 1 a day is not too much. Start them on a good domain name. Pack them with contents,Ã‚ try to write as many post as possible till you run dry of content before your lunch. Always have a sitemap and submit them to the search engines. Always have some kind of tracking code like Google analytics where you are to track keywords and popular post. Once you get a couple of blog, you can make money by doing paid posts, selling links or ad space, or you can sell the entire blog.

Just in case you are wondering I run 7 blogs now. Its definitely more profitable than when I am running one.

A 12-week Plan for More Bike Power

Jan 22nd, 2008 | Comments

One of my weakness is not able to ride strong. When it gets down to crunch time my legs felt like jelly. The only way I am going to improve this season is a giant leap through biking. Recently I have a strain near my groin area which has slowed down my running and swimming. Old man are starting to zoom pass me at my regular running route. I hope that this 12 week plan will put some power back into my legs.

Following is a 12-week schedule from triathletemag.com of neuromuscular-training workouts that are designed to:
1) increase your muscle-activation potential during cycling and then;
2) increase the endurance characteristics of your newly recruited fast-twitch fibers.

Be sure to warm up thoroughly before doing these high-intensity efforts and to cool down thoroughly afterwards. The 30-second hill sprints should be performed on the steepest hill you can find. The one-minute hill sprints may be done on a shallower hill and will be done at a slightly lower intensity. Just spin slowly for recovery. Every fourth week is a scaled-back recovery week to prevent overtraining. By the end of week 12 you’ll be ready to race—with more muscle than ever.

Week 1: 4 x 30-sec. hill sprints w/ 2-min. active recoveries
Week 2: 6 x 30-sec. hill sprints w/ 2-min. active recoveries
Week 3: 8 x 30-second hill sprints with 2-minute active recovery
Week 4: (Recovery) 6 x 30-second hill sprints with 2-minute active recovery
Week 5: 6 x 1-minute hill sprints with 2-minute active recovery
Week 6: 8 x 1-minute hill sprints with 2-minute active recovery
Week 7: 10 x 1-minute hill sprints with 2-minute active recovery
Week 8: (Recovery) 6 x 1-minute hill sprints with 2-minute active recovery
Week 9: 3 x 3 minutes @ 20km time-trial intensity with 3-minute active recovery
Week 10: 4 x 3 minutes @ 20km time-trial intensity with 3-minute active recovery
Week 11: 5 x 3 minutes @ 20km time-trial intensity with 3-minute active recovery
Week 12: (Recovery) 3 x 3 minutes @ 20km time-trial intensity with 3-minute active recovery
