How to Use LinkedIn Groups to Get More Clients

Jan 16th, 2014 | Comments

Most people just waste time on LinkedIn, browsing randomly and sending out odd connection requests hoping to land a jobs. Most of them are looking the wrong way. The secret is in LinkedIn Groups. LinkedIn Groups are a very good way to get more clients for your freelance business. LinkedIn has over 200 million users, most groups have at least a thousand members and you can tap into this crowd to find your target customers. There always people looking to solve a problem. Join groups related to your industries and participate in the problem solving not join random discussion, and soon you be the expert of the group and most people will go directly to you. If you have optimizing your LinkedIn profile according to what you are offfering, you get a steady flow of job offers.

Creating a Reliable Lead Generation Channel for Your Freelancing Business

Jan 14th, 2014 | Comments

When I started freelancing I did not have a constant flow of clients. I search job boards, forums and classified ads, there were not many jobs and it was hard to convert those prospects into clients. I didn’t know what to do.

Since then I have gather a few methods from various freelancer and marketers I came across that have work well.

Define The Types of Client

For me a good client will be someone for pays $5000 or more for a single project. Loosely translate to $500 per man-day charge. Anything less are just not worth the time and effort even if I have no income. Recurring revenue is another thing that I would focus on. Clients I take on should be coming back to me for more work.

Lead Source

Leads can be generated through: 1. On-line classified ads. This are slow, and may generate 1 or 2 leads per month, but maintenances is easy. Most of the ads run for a month, then you go back and turn it on again. Use Google calender to set reminders, keep the url in the calender, so when an reminder is send, all you need to do is click the url. 2. Blog It takes a lot effort to get ranked in the search engine. Some SEO knowledge required here 3. PPC Paid Ads Using Adwords and facebook ads can generate the fastest and most relevant leads. 4. Linkedin People are always looking for someone good in certain skill set. Setting up a good profile can help generate a fair amount of traffic

Building the list

Now you have gather all the source, you need channel them to a place where you can keep build relationships. Use an opt-in list builder like mailchimp. Set up a landing page to catch all that traffic. Set up an series of emails.

Other more manual generation

Responding to wanted ads, forum query and user groups and posting links back to your blog post which eventually end up on your landing page.
