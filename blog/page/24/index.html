7 Tips on Training for Your 2.4km Ran

Oct 15th, 2006

Your birthday is coming soon.
You are reminded to take of your IPPT Test.
You have been working for a few years, life is good to you, you put on a couple of pounds.
Your job have occupied most of your time, sleep is next, the rest is split between family and your TV.
As the dateline draws near, you bought out that hidden pair of sports shoe. Oh it look so new. The last time you have put it on was on your test day!
You determine to take your pet event, the 2.4km ran, head on.
You head towards the nearest track, and off you go…well at least for a good 100m, before that retiree over took you, that preschool giggles when he zoom past you, and even that friendly neighborhood chiwawa proudly make her strides pass you.

You went pass the 200m.
You are penting, breathing hard, you stop for a breather an decides you too tired and you will perform better tomorrow when you are better rested.

So what when wrong?

Here’s the checklist:
1.Excerise regularly…nope
2.Healthy diet…….never
3.Manage to maintain body weight….er
4….

Well here’s the super formula for acing your ran without even putting in an effort, well at least not much.

  1. Get a Medical examination
    If have gain weight over the past year, between 2-5kg you should get yourself examine. Why? Weight gain is disease associated. High cholesterol level, high blood pressure, hypertension are very early signs…you have a heart attack soon. You have heard about runners dying of heart attacks. Don’t be one play it safe. Get the all clear from your doctor.

2.Changing your eating habits
To ran fast you have to be lighter. That’s means no oily stuff, less carbohydrate like noodles, white bread and rice. Split your meals to 6 a day. Breakfast, Morning snack , lunch, Afternoon snack, dinner, night snacks. A good way is to snack on fruit bars and reduce lunch and dinner intake. No eating after 8pm.

  1. Set a goal
    Most of the time is to make the min required time. You need to know whets yours. If you don’t, how can you make that cut?

  2. The schedule. Start your ran with a walk
    If you have not being running, you walk. You train 3x a week. Build up your distance from 400m.

5.Hydration
Always replace the amount water you have loss during your workout. If you raining on a track or treadmill, stop to drink every round or drink while you ran. Hydration prevents fratigue, the most important and neglected part of training. if you are not tired you will improve faster. So drink up. A good way to meaure is to weight before your ran and after. The differences in weight is the amount you need. You can do this in a gym.

  1. Attires
    A good pair of running shoes can improve your performances. Most shops have professional to check your feet, anaysle your rans to find you a proper fitting shoe. Get at least 2 pair of nice running singlet and shorts. choose a lighter material with good ventilation. Always put it on and look at yourself in the mirror. If you look good, you ran good.

7.Finally and the most important lesson not taught in schools during your physical fitness lessons. The correct running technique.
Observe how a world class marathoner rans and look at the average runner in the park.
You find that the average runner, moves almost everypart of their body, excessive side-to-side motion, or swinging across the body, while a world class runner is so smooth and more efficient.

So how do you ran?
I will discuss only movement of the arms here which is more than enough.

  1. Swing forwards a straight line.

  2. Keep your elbows in and held low.

  3. As you run, swing your arms in a relaxed manner, like a pendulum.

So good luck adn train safely
