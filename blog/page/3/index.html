Portable Skype

Mar 12th, 2009

I been a big fan of portable programs ever since I wipe out accidentally my firefox 2 years ago. I was looking for a way not to reinstall my applications and to make them go on my usb. I been using a couple of such program Firefox, Thunderbird, coreftp, putty, keeppass, xampp and many more. The latest addition is Skype.
Download Squad show as how

  1. Install Skype on your PC and then find the installation folder. It’s probably something like “C:Program FilesSkype.” Copy the Skype.exe file to a folder on your USB flash drive.  

  2. In that same folder on your flash drive, create a subfolder called “data.”

  3. Create a new text file called “Skype.bat” or Skype Portable.bat” or whatever you like.

  4. Open your new file using Notepad or another text editor and write “skype.exe /datapath:”data” /removable” (without the quotes).

  5. Save and close your file

How to Know if You Are Ready for Your Niche

Mar 12th, 2009

Your gone through some steps or routine to come to a conclusion on your niche for your website, blog or article campaign. You are all set and passionate about your niche and ready to unleash it on the www audiences. Now before you do that and possible end up with a dead campaign or blog, do a simple test to make sure you are ready for it. Write 20 articles on your niche without any references material, simply using your own words and knowledge. Once your are done spin 5 sub articles out each of this 20 articles. If you are able to do that you have a year worth of content and probably more. Now for a little link muscle. Use 20 on your blog and each of the 5 spin article on a sub domain blog linking to its main. For article campaign simple submit 20 articles to 1 main article directory and separate the 5 across an other directory linking to the main article. Now you have something worthwhile to keep going.

PPC Tips

Dec 14th, 2008

I recently moved to my new place, its good to move out of my parent’s place after 35 years. The move took longer than expected about 2 months actually. All this well I have been able to do any actual work on my websites. Most of my website are simply sold as domains. I have been focusing intensely on PPC. I have managed to create profitable campaigns after studying lots of materials from various gurus. I have formatted my own sets of rules.
1. Create a system. Whether its niche research, finding keywords, locating products or setting up campaign. Create a system where you are able to go through the same process again for each new project. You might start with some else system but on the way refine it. Be prepare to throw away what your believes or what you have learn and accept new ideas.
2. Always go deep on keywords. One of the most valuable lesson I have learn is to go deep. People search for phrase when they are seeking solution, they are looking for specific solution so your keyword needs to cater these searches.
3. Measurement. I believe in measuring and keeping scores. Always have tracking codes install. Always check your campaign. Always keep records, create a excel if you have to, to monitor your profits.
4. Never turn off a campaign, searches fluctuates. Whats not popular might be in the future.
5. Out bid your opponent, what PPC was create for, is for business to out bid each other. Most rookie make the mistake of bidding too low. Don’t be afraid to bid for keywords that cost more than a dollar.
