Home Office

Jun 8th, 2008

There are a few articles on home office in the newspaper. Most of it revolve around creating a structure and being discipline. It felt like these people are creating a environment similar to a job except that you are at home. The main reason we set up home office are because we want freedom, work when we please, and we feel like it. Creating a structure? If i need structure and discipline I would get a job or get a real office.

Furnishing your home office.
If you are starting out, I would minimize and improvise on furniture. If you Spend $3000 on new equipment, it will eat into your profit, advertisings or marketing cost. I would get better equipment when my business picks up, and add more on the way. That way I spending what I can afford.

Meeting client
It would be easier to meet the client at their place, rather than have it the other way around. at the same time allows you to get some fresh air.

What matter most is ”Its your rules!”.

Facebook Open Platform Released

Jun 3rd, 2008

Facebook just released Facebook Platform as Open Source. Facebook Open Platform is a snapshot of the infrastructure that runs Facebook Platform. It includes the API infrastructure, the FBML parser, the FQL parser, and FBJS, as well as implementations of many common methods and tags.

Download Facebook Open Platform here. The fb-open-platform.tar.gz archive contains the tools necessary to implement Facebook Open Platform – including the API, FBML (Facebook Markup Language), FBJS (Facebook JavaScript), and FQL (Facebook Query Language) – in your own environment. It also contains libfbml-1.2.0.tar.gz, which contains the essential libraries for parsing and rendering FBML.

How Did I Survive 6 Month Without a Job

Jun 2nd, 2008

I get a lot of people asking me how did I survive 6 month without a job. First of all I got to make it clear, I did not have a regular job but I have several source of income. I have a internet business, a few traditional business and I taught martial arts on a 1 on 1 basis. Although the combine income is less than a thousand but it was more than enough for my survive. There are several ways to cut back on daily expenses as well. I made a list here.

  1. Track your expense down to the tiniest detail. Record what you paid for allows for comparison later, which helps to find the cheapest source.

  2. Eat cheaply, instant noodles, fired rice, bread, potatoes etc all those that are filling and comes n large portions.

  3. Skip the public transport, run, walk or bike instead.

  4. Always allow your friends to pay for your food, and accept their second hand stuffs.

These are just tip of the ice berg, I wonder if anyone have any interesting ideas to share?
