How to Be a Successful Entrepreneur

Feb 19th, 2008

Wikipedia describe entrepreneur as An entrepreneur is a person who operates a new enterprise or venture and assumes some accountability for the inherent risks. If you have line up all the successful entrepreneur in the world and tried to profile them, you find that they all different. From the size, looks, to the way they think and operate. There are no fixed rule to become an entrepreneur, but at some point you be required to take risks or perform and work in a way that most peers will not. If you go back to all the successful entrepreneurs and ask them for the key to their success, you probably get as many as the number of people you asked. How do become successful? We do it by failing more than others and we get up each time. Technical skills like marketing, writing business plan, managing can be learn but attitude and commitment are forged through countless hardship, visualization, developing passion and undying attitude. What the most important thing you can do to be a successful entrepreneur is simply “Don’t Quit”

WordPress 2.3.3 Urgent Security Release

Feb 6th, 2008

If you are using WordPress 2.3.2 and have registration enabled, pay attention. What Are the Three Key Characteristics of an Entrepreneur?

Feb 5th, 2008

I define entrepreneur as someone who have control over their own future. Every entrepreneur is different. Each has their own set of strengths, weaknesses, talents and abilities. However, entrepreneurs who have stood the test of time and have made a difference do share common characteristics. What words do you associate with an entrepreneur? Passion, vision, leadership, commitment?

What are the three key characteristics of an entrepreneur? 1.Vision Entrepreneurs provides a sense of direction by being the light at the end of the tunnel. Their the guiding stars when everyone else are lost. Vision helps the entrepreneur to define their goals. Vision is used to clearly communicated the goals to the stakeholders and must motivate them. To be successful the entrepreneur first needs to understand why these stakeholders will find the vision appealing and benefits them. Once the vision is in place the entrepreneur can start work on the business plan.

2.High risk-taker Entrepreneurs are constantly moving and exploring uncharted waters, redefining, improving certain traits that often treated as risk by conventional business owner. Entrepreneurs understands the need for breakthroughs and in order to break new grounds, calculated risk must be taken. More often the higher the risk, the higher the rewards or failure. Entrepreneurs allow themselves to fail and learn form past experiences.

3.Hard work A recent study show that the average number of hours put in from an entrepreneurs amount to 60 hours per week or more. However, I believe that’s the minimum. An entrepreneurs is constantly working, their are loaded with issues and every minute is spent trying to solve a problem.

How Many Blog Can You Write On?

Jan 30th, 2008

There are lots of debate on the number of blog one should have. Most successful bloggers are likely to write only one blog and it seems like you only need one blog to be profitable. What happens when your blog did not take off? Maybe there are no readers, not enough traffic, or simply a bad niche? I do believe that if you work hard on one blog alone, you eventually get there, but what about when you have not make it yet. Your blog have too little traffic or readers to sell advertisement and your Adsense earning is only a few cents for entire month.

When I started, I started 5 blogs and only kept 2. Overtime I have started new blogs, keep some going and shutdown some. I was working on a full-time job back then, I still found time to work each blog daily. There are many benefits of starting multiple blogs. You have a sharper learning curve, you save time by experimenting different things on different blogs. After a period of time, you will shutdown the less preforming ones and work on the better performing ones. You choose to let this discarded blog float, where you might find some use for them in the future or use it to link to some of your websites, you might even be able to sell links or advertising spaces on them.

While most of my blog were started with free host, I have found that time and time again that if you host your own blog, you get better traffic fast. I have one blog lunch that will gain organic traffic the next day. Of course there a lot more SEO tactics involved but then again you can’t use them on free hosts like blogger.

I doÃ‚ recommend you start as many blogs as you can, even 1 a day is not too much. Start them on a good domain name. Pack them with contents,Ã‚ try to write as many post as possible till you run dry of content before your lunch. Always have a sitemap and submit them to the search engines. Always have some kind of tracking code like Google analytics where you are to track keywords and popular post. Once you get a couple of blog, you can make money by doing paid posts, selling links or ad space, or you can sell the entire blog.

Just in case you are wondering I run 7 blogs now. Its definitely more profitable than when I am running one.
