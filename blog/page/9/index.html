How to Be a Successful Entrepreneur

Feb 19th, 2008

Wikipedia describe entrepreneur as An entrepreneur is a person who operates a new enterprise or venture and assumes some accountability for the inherent risks. If you have line up all the successful entrepreneur in the world and tried to profile them, you find that they all different. From the size, looks, to the way they think and operate. There are no fixed rule to become an entrepreneur, but at some point you be required to take risks or perform and work in a way that most peers will not. If you go back to all the successful entrepreneurs and ask them for the key to their success, you probably get as many as the number of people you asked. How do become successful? We do it by failing more than others and we get up each time. Technical skills like marketing, writing business plan, managing can be learn but attitude and commitment are forged through countless hardship, visualization, developing passion and undying attitude. What the most important thing you can do to be a successful entrepreneur is simply “Don’t Quit”

Fastest Way to Lose Weight

Feb 9th, 2008

The problem most people face when starting a weight lose workout is consistency. Most people set unrealistic goals and when they don’t reach them, it’s an excuse to give up. New research out of the University of California at Berkeley of more than 20,000 runners found that the people who quit on or took “breaks” from their fitness plans put on abnormal amounts of weight that they couldn’t easily knock off. So stick to your plan. No Excuses!

PUMP IT OUT
Weight training are the best fat basters and even cardio can’t match it. A research from Boston University shows that muscles developed through strength and weight training, burn fat as readily as hours on an elliptical.

GET FIT IN JUST 3 WORKOUTS A WEEK
Nutrition is more important than your exercise habits. You are what you eat. Make sure you stick with whole, natural foods, such as fruits, vegetables, nuts, fish, and protein to set the foundation. For your exercise sessions, focus on quality and intensity, not quantity. Exercising 3 days a week for only 45 minutes or less. Strengthen your entire body with multi-muscle resistance exercises and burn fat by using short-burst interval training. Follow that up with stretches for tight muscle groups only.
